Introduction

  • This study investigatese the violatility of different voter types using Taiwan 2024 elections data.
  • Specifically, we analyze how and why voters switch between presidential and legislative elections.
  • We compare convententional decision model (logistic regression) with machine learning models (random forest and XGBoost) to understand what factors are most important in predicting voting patterns.
  • Loyalists are defined as those who vote in both presidential and legislative elections over time (2020, 2024), while non-loyalists are those who switch.
  • On the right hand side, we focus on media effect or TV habits with democraphic variables.

Motivation

  • Public opinion is the foundation of a democratic system.

  • As democratic societies are getting more polarized and complicated, social engineering via different media prompts voters to face more cognitive-costly decisions.

  • With the introduction of Taiwan Peopleâ€™s Party (TPP) in 2020, the party system has become more complicated.

  • The key question is the causal path of change: is the new party driving public opinion change or the public opinion driving the parties and party system to change?

  • In this study, we start from the latter: how do voters change their voting patterns in the 2024 elections?

  • Caveat: we do not conduct the causal analysis in this study. Instead, we focus on what is likely to prompt voters to change.

  • Lesson: how to improve public opinion polling to more effective predict voting turnout and choices.

  • Models: Logistic regression, random forest, XGBoost.

  • ROC and Predictive Accuracy

    • Suggests important variance in voter switching remains unobserved.

  • Top Predictors of Switching

    • Age: High volatility among young voters.
    • National Identity: Weak identity points to switching
    • Media Use:
      • Viewership of SET and FTV (pro-DPP) linked to loyalty.
      • TVBS viewers more likely to split or switch.
      • No TV: Non-loyalists more likely to abstain.
    • Tondu Stance:
      • Ambiguous/no stance also linked to non-loyalism.

Implications for Public Opinion Research

  • Media polarization continues to structure Taiwanâ€™s partisan dynamics.
  • Television remains influential: platform matters for loyalty formation.
  • Generational divide is crucial:
    • Age strongly conditions party behavior.
    • TPP support among youth vs. KMT/DPP among older cohorts.
  • Survey design should capture identity nuance and media exposure more precisely.

Future Plans and Next Steps

  • Synthetic cohort modeling: Agent-based modeling
  • **Silicon sampling* to simulate voter behavior with synthetic data.
  • Deploy interpretable ML models (e.g., SHAP for XGBoost) to deepen insight.
  • Explore causal inference: Use of natural experiments or quasi-experimental designs to establish causality.
  • New survey design: sampling techniques targetting younger and non-partisan voters.
